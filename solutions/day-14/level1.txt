#Q 01] What is component life cycles?
  - the process of mountion, updating, unmounting component from the React app.

#Q 02] What is the purpose of life cycles?
  - lifecycle stages allow developers to initialte various methods for interaction, based on the component existance.

#Q 03] What are the three stages of a component life cycle?
  - mount, update, unmount

#Q 04] What does mounting means?
  - the component rendering into the DOM

#Q 05] What does updating means?
  - the component updating throughout component's state changes.

#Q 06] What does unmounting means?
  - removing the component from the DOM
  
#Q 07] What is the most common built-in mounting life cycle method?
  - componentDidMount();

#Q 08] What are the mounting life cycle methods?
  - constructor()
  - static getDerivedStateFromProps()
  - render()
  - componentDidMount()

#Q 09] What are the updating life cycle methods?
  - static getDerivedStateFromProps()
  - shouldComponentUpdate()
  - render()
  - getSnapshotBeforeUpdate()
  - componentDidUpdate()

#Q 10] What is the unmounting life cycle method?
  - componentWillUnmount()
